See Pittsburgh

🛶     

Explore this underrated city! Fingers crossed for good weather because walking around this city is glorious. If the weather doesn't cooperate, there's plenty to see indoors too.

Explore

  • Ride the Duquesne Incline: If you only do one thing in Pittsburgh, this should be it! This inclined cable car transported miners over Mount Washington before there was a tunnel through this massive face of earth, and it has been preserved in its original operational form. The bottom of the incline is walkable from downtown over a bridge, and the ride up takes you to the best views of the city.
  • Walk, Run, or Bike the Riverfront Trails: What makes Pittsburgh Pittsburgh is its three rivers. There is an abundance of riverfront trails along all three rivers, including access from downtown. Rent a bike to explore some or all 24 miles, including through Point State Park and by our sports fields, PNC Park and Heinz Field.
  • Kayak on the Rivers: Get an even closer experience with the rivers with this nearby kayak rental stand, just minutes of rowing from the intersection of the three rivers.
  • Schenley Park: A huge park with plenty of hills and trails. Phipps Conservatory and Carnegie Mellon University are both adjacent to Schenley.
  • Frick Park: Another densely green park within minutes of downtown.

Stops

  • The Andy Warhol Museum: One of the four Carnegie Museums of Pittsburgh, this one is devoted to this iconic Pittsburgher.
  • Carnegie Museum of Natural History: This other of the Carnegie Museums of Pittsburgh is one of the country's top natural history museums.
  • Carnegie Museum of Art: Right next to the Carnegie Museum of Natural History, this is a great art museum.
  • Carnegie Science Center: The science center is right by downtown along the river trails, and it includes a submarine docked in the river for you to board and explore.
  • The Mattress Factory: A museum of alternative art.

Food

  • Church Brew Works: Eat dinner and drink beer from kegs on the altar of the beautiful former St. John's the Baptist.
  • Apteka: One of the tastiest restaurants we know in Pittsburgh. Period. Amazing Polish food for all to try, though the entire menu is also vegan!
  • Pusadee's Garden: A great restaurant in the heart of the Lawrenceville neighborhood.
  • Soju: Tasty Korean cuisine.
  • The Milkshake Factory: A must-do for chocolate lovers -- gourmet chocolates and delicious shakes and sundaes. There are several locations throughout Pittsburgh, including one at the heart of downtown.
  • Primanti Brothers: A greasy, Pittsburgh classic, featuring sandwiches famously with french fries in them, originally designed to be convenient for truckers to consume with one hand while driving.
  • Pamela's: A famous breakfast diner with vintage vibes. Fun fact: President Obama always requested to eat here when he visited Pittsburgh.
  • Eleven: A "contemporary kitchen" in the Strip District near the hotel blocks.
  • Grand Concourse: A fancy restaurant in the preserved grand train station on the south side of the city.
  • Monterey Bay: This sits at the top of Mount Washington (where the Duquesne Incline takes you) with stunning city views.
  • Altius: Another fancy restaurant at the top of Mount Washington.
